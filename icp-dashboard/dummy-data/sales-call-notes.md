# Sales-call notes (dummy data)

Rough notes from recorded calls. This is where the buying motivation lives. Phone calls stay
irreplaceable: AI tells you who to call, these tell you why they buy. Names map to `customers.csv`.

---

**Call — Jordan Reyes (BrightPath Agency), 32 min**
- Runs a 1-person agency, 8 active client retainers, been at it 4 years.
- "I'm not buying software, I'm buying margin. Every lifetime deal I resell into a retainer
  pays for itself in a month and prints after that."
- Biggest fear: overhead. "The day I have to hire is the day my margin dies."
- Buys 2-3 deals a month, has for years. Doesn't evaluate much if the price is one-time.
- What he wants: tools he can put a logo on and resell. Reliability over features.

**Call — Marisol Vega (Delta Creative), 27 min**
- Solo operator, "nine hats," 12-ish clients.
- "If it saves me from hiring, it's worth it. I'd rather own 30 tools than manage one person."
- Decides fast. Cares about whether she'll still use it in 6 months, not the feature list.

**Call — Sofia Marin (BrightNest), 19 min**
- In-house, only marketer, reports to CEO.
- "Monday is reporting hell. I'm in spreadsheets till noon and then someone asks why a number
  changed."
- Wants speed to a clean number she can defend. Not price-sensitive, time-sensitive.

**Call — Sam Okafor (Driftboard), 11 min**
- Early founder, "just exploring." Bought on price, low intent.
- Not a fit for the high-value avatar. Useful as a contrast segment.
