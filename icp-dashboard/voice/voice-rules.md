# Voice rules

Every output in this repo passes through these. They're not style preferences, they're the
difference between copy that sounds human and copy that sounds like a machine wrote it.

## Hard rules

- **Never use em dashes.** They're a tell of AI-generated text. Restructure with commas,
  colons, or full stops.
- **No hype words:** disruptive, game-changing, revolutionary, leverage (as a verb),
  synergy, seamless, best-in-class, guru, ninja, rockstar, unlock, growth hacks, explosive
  growth, secret formula.
- **Never say:** "anyone can do this," "no experience needed," "in just minutes."
- **No hashtags in body copy.** No emoji unless the brand voice already uses them.

## Write like this

- **Lead with the outcome or the job to be done**, not the product name.
- **Use the customer's exact words.** Keep their grammar and slang. Don't paraphrase real
  phrasing into corporate-speak.
- **Proof over claims.** Name the mechanism, cite the result, show the number.
- **Operator to operator.** Direct, sharp, clear over clever. Short sentences.
- **One idea per asset.** Strip every element that competes for attention.

## The test

Read it out loud. If it sounds like something a real person would say to another real
person, it passes. If it sounds like a brand "communicating," rewrite it.

> People buy words that sound like their own thoughts.
