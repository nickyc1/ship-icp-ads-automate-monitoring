# Benchmarks and thresholds

These are the example thresholds the `Analyze` node uses. They are starting points from
running real accounts, not laws. Tune them to your margins and your sales cycle.

## Profit-to-spend targets

| Platform | Target value/spend | Why |
|----------|--------------------|-----|
| Google   | **1.5** | High-intent search should clear a comfortable margin |
| Meta     | **0.5** | Demand-gen runs leaner; it earns its keep over a longer window |

A campaign is graded **green** at or above its target, **red** below. Red doesn't mean
kill it tomorrow — it means it goes to the top of the "needs attention" list.

## CAC vs LTV

Don't judge a campaign on CAC alone. A higher CAC that brings higher-LTV buyers wins.
Use a breakeven horizon (Nick uses ~3 months) and ask: *do these buyers pay back within it?*
A $200 CAC into a high-LTV cohort beat a $75 CAC into a low-LTV one by 4x.

## Anomaly flags

- **CPA spike** — CPA more than **2x** its historical norm.
- **Spend pacing** — spend running below the mid-afternoon baseline (it won't deliver the day).
- **Frequency** (Meta) — climbing frequency with falling CTR is creative fatigue.

## The decision language

The digest never just reports numbers. It says what to do:

- **Scale** — green and underfunded (low spend, real conversions).
- **Cut** — high spend, low conversion.
- **Investigate or pause** — below target.
- **Hold** — at target, fully funded.

> ROAS in Google Ads is not profit. Your business runs on profit. Reconcile platform
> numbers against first-party data before you trust the story.
